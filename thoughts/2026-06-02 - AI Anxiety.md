# AI Anxiety

In regards to the development of AI systems, I first had my existential crisis when GPT-3 was released. I've had an amateur AI/ML for a while now, and in general just how the human brain/mind works. I personally think there is nothing "magical" or "supernatural" about the brain. The brain is a large biological neural network with lots of computation capacity. Due to its neurons, architecture, and such, the brain implements to learn things in a certain way and fast. It seemd obvious to me that AI systems would eventually be able to do what the brain does (computationally) and very likely go way beyond, and it was at that moment where I felt people had cracked an important piece to the development of such AI systems and it became viscerally obvious to me where things were heading and they were heading there fast.

This reminds me of anxiety you may experience before surgery. There is the first anxiety where you realize you have to have surgery and you set things into motion that will result in such a surgery some time from now, and then the second anxiety when the day of surgery has arrived.

The first such anxiety I experienced was around the release of GPT-3.

Today I am experience the second kind of anxiety as large parts of what I had previously done at work can be automated by Codex and Claude Code. I think the eventual goals of these companies is to create AI systems that are cognitively more capable than all humans and cheaper and faster, so any job that is mostly cognitive in nature is at risk. At some point more capable robotic systems come online (I estimate withing 5 to 10 years) that can start to replace physical work that people do. At that point we've effectively reached a point where computers/machines have completely replaced the need of humans in both the cognitive domain and physical domain. This would be an enormous achievement by humans but it begs the question where does it leave us?

Most optimism I see is based on some sort of copium such as that there is something magical about humans (which is not the case), or these AI systems "run into a wall", i.e. their increase in capabilities halts, which there is no evidence for, and such.

But in this transitional time things will get very interesting and very weird.
