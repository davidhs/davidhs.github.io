# Meditation related exercise

Here is a meditation related exercise.

Take a bowl of water and fill it to the brim.

Walk around with it. Back and forward. Turn around. And so on and so forth.

The goal is not to spill any water yet move around.

Notice how the center of your visual attention, the "spotlight" is on the bowl.

Also notice how you take on the visual periphary to avoid colliding into anything.

If you are in a crowded space you will notice how these qualities have to interact with each other to accomplish the task.

This is related to awareness. If we split awareness into attention (the spotlight) and peripheral awareness (the periphery, or background), then the attention is on the bowl and the peripheral awareness on everything else.  If this qualities of mind don't work together you will "spill water from the bowl".
