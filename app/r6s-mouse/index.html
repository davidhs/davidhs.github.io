Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six Siege

This document assumes you're using Windows and have raw mouse input enabled.

For maximum precision you want your in-game sensitivity to be as low as possible. Then use your mouse DPI to compensate for the rotation speed.

Turn circumference is the number you wish to calculate.

Terminology

  • CPI: counts per inch, the number of steps the mouse will report to the OS when it moves one inch.
    • Often erroneously referred to as DPI or dots per inch.
  • DPI: dots per inch, or how many dots per inch an injet printer is capable of.
    • Usually referring to CPI
  • Inch: 1 inch is exactly 2.54 cm.
  • Turn Circumference: the distance you need to move your mouse to complete a full rotation. Measured in cm/rev.

Formula

TurnCircumference = 2.54 * (100 * 100 * 2 * PI) / (CPI * MouseAxisSensitivity * MouseSensitivity * MouseSensitivityMultiplierUnit)

  • TurnCircumference: how far you need to move your mouse in cm to complete a full revolution in-game.
  • PI: constant, about 3.14.
  • CPI: CPI or DPI of your mouse, e.g. 400.
  • MouseAxisSensitivity: Either Mouse Sensitivity Horizontal or Mouse Sensitivity Vertical, a value from 1 to 100.
  • MouseSensitivity: default value is 50. Not possible to change in-game.
  • MouseSensitivityMultiplierUnit: default value is 0.020000. Not possible to change in-game.
We can simplify the formula by using the default values you cannot change in-game.

TurnCircumference = 159592.90680236148 / (CPI * MouseAxisSensitivity)

Notes

  • The smallest angle you can move with no modifier (in-game sensitivity of 100) is about 0.58 degrees, and with in-game sensitivity of 1 would be 0.0058 degrees or 0.35 minutes of arc. (With 20/20 vision you're able to resolve spatial pattern by a visual angle of 1 minute of arc)
